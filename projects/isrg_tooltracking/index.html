What is Tool Tracking - Sensor Fusion of Vision and Kinematics

Tool tracking is about real-time locating precisely where are the surgical instruments in the surgical field during surgery. It is an old topic, even back then, for surgical robotics with many potential applications such as collision avoidance when instruments are not within the field of view or hidden beneath the tissues. If accurate enough, it could even become a handy measurement tool.

For surgical robotics application, tool tracking presented both a challenge and an opportunity. The challenge is to reliablly locate the instrument with good accuracy in a complex and dynamic surgical field. The opportunity is the availability of the precise robotic kinematics that could be biased. Hence, an efficient sensor fusion mechanism like extended Kalman Filter can be applied to correct the kinematics biases in real-time based on vision observation. The vision observation is 3D location of the tracked instrument based on matching its left and right views and the endoscopic stereo camera calibration.

System Evaulation - Performance Evaluation and Clinical Labs

One big challenge to push technology advancement into medicine, including surgery, is to prove that the proposed technology is 1) firstly safe, and 2) secondly, effective for the proposed objectives.

For tool tracking development, even just a technical development, we went through objective evaulation of its performance with dry labs and clinical labs with surgeons. For the objective performance evaluation in real-time, we built an efficient pipeline with NDI's 6-DOF optical tracker sevring as the ground-truth tracker.
