What is Active Stereo

Active stereo is a technique used in computer vision and depth sensing where two (or more) cameras are paired with an active illumination source, such as an infrared (IR) projector. Different from traditional passive stereo, the projector projects a known pattern onto the scene, which helps the cameras solve correspondence problems even when the environment lacks texture or lighting contrast — yielding more reliable depth maps. Well-known active stereo systems include Intel's Real Sense. Compared to ToF (Time-of-Flight) sensors such as Microsft's Kinect 2, active stereo cameras also provide good quality images for purposes beyond depth such as object detection and tracking.

Back in 2014-15, after researching I learnd its unique advantages including being compact & efficient, uniform & speckle-Reduced IR Projection of high-density patterns.
